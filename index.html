THUAPR 3
Arrival Night
Landing at 11pm · pick up car · hotel
Thursday, April 3
✈️

Bienvenidos a Cuscatlán!

Land at Monseñor Óscar Arnulfo Romero International Airport (SAL) around 11pm. Grab the rental car and drive ~30 min to the InterContinental in Metrocentro. Tonight is rest — big adventures start mañana. Rebe, welcome to Rafa's homeland! 🌋

💡 Quick Tips

Download Waze/Google Maps offline before landing. Highway from airport is well-lit and easy at night. Keep small bills ($1s and $5s) handy for pupusas, tips, and street vendors.

FRIAPR 4
San Salvador Highlights
Centro Histórico · Casa Bou · Los Planes · Rainbow Slide · Date Night
Friday, April 4
🏛️

Morning: Centro Histórico

Start at the heart of San Salvador. Walk Plaza Libertad with its colonial architecture. Visit Iglesia El Rosario — looks like a concrete bunker outside, but inside rainbow light pours through stunning stained glass. One of the most beautiful churches in Central America. Walk past the Catedral Metropolitana and Palacio Nacional. Rebe is going to love this.

culture
☕

Mid-Morning: Starbucks Casa Bou

THE Starbucks everyone talks about. On Calle Rubén Darío in the historic center, Casa Bou is inside a restored 1920s Art Nouveau building. Stained glass by Salvadoran artist Margarita Llort tells the coffee story from seed to cup. Murals by Darwin Flores and Madjer Linares cover the walls. First flagship Starbucks community coffeehouse in all of Latin America. Try the Pacamara coffee — grown on volcanic soil right here in El Salvador.

culturecoffee
🚗 ~30 min uphill
🏔️

Afternoon: Los Planes de Renderos & Puerta del Diablo

Drive up to Los Planes de Renderos, a.k.a. "Pupusa Mountain." Cool breezy hilltop famous for dozens of pupuserías with insane city views. Continue to Puerta del Diablo (Devil's Door) — three massive rock formations at 1,131m elevation. See Lago de Ilopango, Volcán San Vicente, and the Pacific Ocean. Explore La Cueva del Diablo caves near the formations. Views are unreal.

🍽️ Eat Here!

El Ático Pupusería — Los Planes. Pupusas $1-2 each. Try garlic, squash, chicharrón with that panoramic view. Also Casa Campo El Mirador for the biggest pupusa in El Salvador. Becky's first pupusa moment — make it count! 🫓

epic viewspupusas
🎢

Late Afternoon: Rainbow Slide (Tobogán Arcoíris)

Stop at Picnic Steak House on the road to El Boquerón. The famous Rainbow Slide — multi-colored giant slide built into the mountainside with San Salvador views. 60 seconds of pure adrenaline! Restaurant has gorgeous sunset views too. Min age 12, sign a waiver, totally safe and fun.

adventure
🌙

Evening: RPRP Date Night

Clean up at the hotel and treat yourselves. San Salvador's food scene has exploded.

🍽️ Date Night Picks

El Xolo — Inside MUNA (anthropology museum). Reinvented Salvadoran cuisine. Native corn, chiltepe sauce, cacao desserts. Recognized by Latin America's 50 Best.

Brutto — Contemporary Asian fusion, rooftop vibes, city views. Truffle butter bread is a must.

La Oveja Negra — Cozy, mysterious ambiance. Creative flavors. Perfect for romance.

Donde Mikel — Basque cuisine. Owner Mikel recommends dishes personally. Incredible steak + seafood.

dinnerdate night

SATAPR 5
Beach Day & Coastal Vibes
El Tunco · Plaza La Libertad · Sunset on the Pacific
Saturday, April 5
🏖️

Morning: El Tunco

Drive west (~45 min) to Playa El Tunco, El Salvador's most famous beach town. Named after a pig-shaped rock offshore. Colorful hostels, surf shops, beachfront restaurants, black volcanic sand. Walk the main strip, breakfast beachside. Water shoes are smart — can be rocky.

beach
🏄

Midday: Surf, Stroll & Seafood

Watch surfers, explore tide pools, photos with the iconic Tunco rock. Walk to Playa El Sunzal next door for quieter vibes. Fresh seafood shacks right on the sand.

🍽️ Eat Here!

Fresh ceviche + fried fish at any beachfront comedor. BocaBoca or Ecobar for nicer sit-down with ocean views. Ceviche de camarón + cold Pilsener = RPRP heaven. 🍺

surfseafood
🚗 ~15 min
⚓

Afternoon: Plaza La Libertad

Drive to Puerto de La Libertad. Renovated Malecón (boardwalk) is perfect for a couples stroll. Browse the artisan market, check the fishing pier, and hit the legendary fish market — pick your fish and they cook it right there.

🍽️ Eat Here!

Fish market — pick your whole fish, shrimp, or lobster. They grill it with sides. Ultra fresh. Also try mariscadas (seafood soups) along the malecón.

foodviews
🌅

Evening: Sunset at El Tunco

Back to El Tunco for sunset — famous for the most beautiful sunsets in Central America. Black sand, crashing waves, fiery sky. Stay for dinner and drinks at beach bars. Live music, fire dancers, fun backpacker energy. Rebe's first El Salvador sunset is going to hit different. 🌅

nightlifesunset

MONAPR 7
Volcán Santa Ana & Lago de Coatepeque
Bucket list hike · Crater lake · Mayan ruins · Return car to airport 8pm
Monday, April 7

⏰ Early Start!

Leave by 5:30-6:00am to reach Cerro Verde National Park early. Hike ~2 hours up. Bring water, sunscreen, hat, snacks, hiking shoes. Trail can be muddy. Return rental car to airport by 8pm — uncle picks you up there and drives you back to the hotel!

🌋

Morning: Volcán Santa Ana (Ilamatepec)

Tallest volcano in El Salvador at 2,381m. Hike through cloud forest ~2 hours and you're rewarded with one of Central America's most stunning views — a brilliant blue-green crater lake inside the volcanic crater. Moderate difficulty. #1 thing to do in El Salvador. Rebe, this is going to blow your mind. 🤯

adventurebucket list
🚗 ~30 min downhill
💎

Afternoon: Lago de Coatepeque

Lago de Coatepeque — turquoise volcanic crater lake formed 50,000+ years ago. Boat ride (~1 hour), jet ski, or relax lakeside with a cold drink. The water color is unreal.

🍽️ Eat Here!

La Pampa Restaurant — Argentinian place on a hill overlooking the lake. Stunning terrace, great steaks + wine. Perfect post-hike reward for RPRP. 🥩🍷

viewslunch
🏛️

Optional: Joya de Cerén (on the way back)

Joya de Cerén — UNESCO World Heritage Site. Mayan farming village buried by volcanic ash ~600 AD. "Pompeii of the Americas." Quick 30-45 min, right off the highway.

Drive the rental back to the airport by 8pm. Uncle meets you there and drives you back to the hotel. Tomorrow: road trip east!

WEDAPR 9
San Miguel Exploration
La Perla del Oriente · Mercado Central · Caves
Wednesday, April 9
🏙️

San Miguel City

Explore "La Perla del Oriente" — El Salvador's second city. Visit the Catedral Basílica Nuestra Señora de la Paz, get lost in the massive Mercado Central (biggest market in El Salvador — save your parking spot!), and check out Teatro Nacional Francisco Gavidia. The central square energy is infectious.

culture
💧

Afternoon: Cuevas de Moncagua

Just 10 minutes from San Miguel! Natural caves with crystal-clear water — locals turned it into a swimming area. Entrance ~$2. Refreshing escape from the heat. Super local vibes.

adventure

🍽️ Must Try!

Panes Migueleños — San Miguel's signature food. Freshly baked bread stuffed with turkey, chicken, or pork, curtido, and special sauces. You cannot get these like this anywhere else. Ask uncle where the best ones are!

Eastern Beaches
Playa El Cuco · Las Flores · La Ventana
Thursday, April 10
🏖️

Playa El Cuco

Most popular beach near San Miguel, ~1.5-2 hours south. Way more laid-back than El Tunco. Great for swimming, beginner surfing, incredible fresh seafood on the sand. Where eastern Salvadorans go for beach days.

beach
🌊

Playa Las Flores

Just down the road — quieter, more upscale, excellent surf breaks. Beautiful black sand, fewer crowds, nice boutique restaurants.

surf

Playa La Ventana — Hidden Gem!

Near Las Flores — visit at low tide to access a cave that opens into a natural stone archway. Looks like a giant window into the Pacific. Incredibly romantic and photogenic. Rafa, this is THE spot to take Rebe for photos. 💕

🇸🇻 Good to Know

Driving: Roads generally good. Night driving fine on highways, cautious on rural roads. Gas is cheap and plentiful.

Safety: El Salvador has improved dramatically. Tourist areas are very safe. Common sense — don't flash expensive stuff, lock car doors.

Tipping: 10% at restaurants. Pupuserías and street food don't require tips.

For Rebe: She's going to love it. Salvadorans are incredibly warm — especially when they find out Rafa has roots there. Expect to be fed constantly. 🫶
